Skillet · Design System

Ember & Iris

A warm ember primary paired with a deep violet interactive accent, plus semantic green and red. Tuned for legibility on a dark terminal background. Click any swatch to copy its hex.

The wordmark

    
Heated iron · vertical gradient #FBD2A0 → #9C441C · ANSI-shadow block

    

      
      
    

    

  


  

    Why it works →
    Amber sits at ~40° on the color wheel; its direct complement is blue (~220°). The two hues flanking that complement — cyan and blue-violet — are its split-complementary partners, giving strong contrast without the vibration of a true opposite. Cyan is the default terminal accent everyone uses, so violet keeps the same color logic while making Skillet distinctive, and bright violet maps cleanly to ANSI magenta in a terminal.
